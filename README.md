# Atalanta

**Atalanta** is an instrumental rock concept album tracing the myth of Atalanta: a woman abandoned at birth, raised in the wild by Artemis, exiled after returning from the Argonaut voyage, and ultimately transformed into a lion forever running.

The album follows her arc from abandonment to freedom. All tracks are in A minor, and all track titles begin with the letter **A**.

## Concept

Instrumental rock concept album tracing a woman's journey from abandonment to freedom through exile, conflict, and transcendence.

## Story

Atalanta was born a girl, not the son her father wanted. So the king abandoned her to die by the mountainside. Her cries drew out a mamma bear. Instead of killing her, the bear took her in and raised her as one of her cubs.

The time came for the cubs to leave their mother. That is when Artemis came: the goddess of the wilderness. Artemis took Atalanta to Arcadia and raised her like a daughter. Atalanta became a swift huntress, a fast runner, and a great warrior.

No one, including the other gods, dared to enter Arcadia. But a man, Hippomenes, wandered there once. He was Atalanta's first contact with the outside world.

When the time came, Artemis sent her champion on a journey with the Argonauts. She warned Atalanta to never marry a man, and to never forget everything she had taught her.

They began their adventure: on sea, on land, to great palaces and great kingdoms, against great warriors and against gods. The Argonauts came back victorious. And Atalanta came back pregnant.

Artemis would not let her set foot in Arcadia. Atalanta had not broken her vow not to marry a man, but she had broken with the Arcadian way of life.

Banished from Arcadia, Atalanta made a home by the mountainside. Hippomenes found her once again. He brought news that her father was looking for his now-famous daughter.

The king organized a foot race to find Atalanta a husband. This would be her very last race.

Meanwhile, Hippomenes sought help from Aphrodite, the goddess of love. During the race, Atalanta was enticed by three golden apples and slowed down. This allowed Hippomenes to win.

They married, but forgot to show gratitude to Aphrodite. So she cursed them. In their accursed state, they provoked the wrath of Rhea, the mother of Zeus. Rhea turned Atalanta and Hippomenes into lions, compelled to run for eternity.

Atalanta was free at last. She would often stop on her track to let Artemis, her goddess mother, pet her.

## Narrative Summary

Atalanta was abandoned by her father for being born a girl. A mother bear raised her until Artemis, goddess of the wilderness, took her to Arcadia and trained her as a huntress. Atalanta joined the Argonauts, but when she returned pregnant, Artemis banished her from Arcadia. She later reunited with Hippomenes, who won her in a foot race with the help of Aphrodite's golden apples. They married but angered the gods, leading to their transformation into lions, forever running. Despite this, Atalanta found freedom and occasional comfort from Artemis.

## Global Constraints

- **Key:** A minor for all tracks.
- **Theme:** The letter **A**. All track titles begin with A.

## Thematic Motifs

- **Artemis theme:** Powerful calling riff anchoring "Artemis" and "Atalanta".
- **Atalanta theme:** Emotional, searching line used in "Abandoned I", "Abandoned II", and "Atalanta".
- **Freedom theme:** Perpetual-motion climax of "Atalanta".

## Instrument Roles

- **Clean guitar:** Memory, innocence.
- **Distorted guitar:** Conflict, will.
- **Rhythm guitar:** Motion, grounding.
- **Bass:** Inevitability, foundation.
- **Drums:** Environment, motion.
