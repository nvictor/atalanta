# Atalanta — Rules & Constraints

## 1. Album Spine (Non-Negotiable)

### 1.1 One-Sentence Purpose
> **Atalanta** is an instrumental rock concept album tracing a woman’s transformation from abandonment to freedom through exile, conflict, and transcendence.

### 1.2 Global Constraints
- **Key Center:** Every track must be in **A Minor**.
- **Naming:** Every track title must start with the letter **A**.

---

## 2. Musical Themes
- **Artemis theme:** Powerful "calling riff" (appears in Artemis, Atalanta).
- **Atalanta theme:** Emotional, searching melody (appears in Abandoned I, Abandoned II, Atalanta).
- **Freedom theme:** Running/perpetual motion (climax of Atalanta).

---

## 3. Track-Level Execution Constraints

### 3.1 Fixed Track Template
Each track is limited to:
- 1 primary riff
- 1 secondary motif
- 1 texture change
- 1 emotional turn

### 3.2 Instrument Role Locking
- **Clean guitar** → memory, innocence
- **Distorted guitar** → conflict, will
- **Rhythm guitar** → motion, grounding
- **Bass** → inevitability, grounding force
- **Drums** → environment and motion, not virtuosity

---

## 4. Naming & Consistency Rules
- Album title: **Atalanta**
- Mood descriptors: maximum **three adjectives per track**
- Groove: one descriptor per track

---

## 5. Definition of “Done”
A track is considered **done** when:
- Structure is fixed
- Motifs are final
- The arrangement plays start‑to‑finish without stopping
